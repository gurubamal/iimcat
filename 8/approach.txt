PRACTICAL CORRECTION BOOST STRATEGY
Opportunity: Stocks that undergo a healthy correction yet have solid fundamentals often stage explosive rebounds (30-50%+ moves). This strategy boosts the ranking of such stocks ahead of the rebound by analyzing their past correction and consolidation patterns.
Core Conditions for Boost
When a stock meets these key conditions, it is primed for a Correction Confidence Boost in its score:
Meaningful Pullback ‚Äì Price is down 10-35% from a recent high (a significant correction, but not a crash).
Oversold Signals ‚Äì Technical indicators flash oversold (e.g. RSI < 35, price near lower Bollinger Band).
Strong Fundamentals ‚Äì Company financials remain robust (profitable, growing earnings, reasonable debt).
Bullish Catalyst ‚Äì A positive development or news score ‚â• 60 suggests a rebound trigger.
Base Formation ‚Äì Bonus: The stock shows signs of consolidation (sideways movement or reduced volatility) after the drop, indicating selling may be exhausted.
‚û°Ô∏è If all the above align, apply a ‚ÄúCorrection Confidence Boost‚Äù to the stock‚Äôs final score. This boost increases the ranking weight for stocks likely to rebound strongly.

Step 1: Detect & Confirm Correction
Use price history to identify a genuine correction, avoiding mere noise or full-on crashes:
correction_pct = ((recent_high - current_price) / recent_high) * 100

# Baseline check for correction size
if 10 <= correction_pct <= 35:
    # Ensure the drop was sustained (not a one-day blip)
    decline_days = count_consecutive_days_down(ticker, window=10)
    # Check for volume spike during sell-off (capitulation signal)
    volume_surge = current_volume / avg_volume_30d
    
    if decline_days >= 5 and volume_surge > 1.3:
        correction_detected = True
        correction_duration = decline_days
    else:
        correction_detected = False  # not a robust correction
else:
    correction_detected = False
Interpretation: We confirm a ‚Äúmeaningful correction‚Äù if the stock fell 10-35% from its high and the decline lasted at least a week with unusually high volume (indicating strong selling pressure that could be capitulating). This filters out shallow dips (<10%) and very severe drops (>35%) that might signal a deeper problem. If the criteria aren‚Äôt met, we skip the boost.
Step 2: Measure Oversold Severity
Evaluate how oversold the stock is on technical grounds. We assign a composite oversold_score (0-100) based on RSI, Bollinger Band position, and volume patterns:
oversold_score = 0

# RSI contribution
if RSI < 25: 
    oversold_score += 30  # extremely oversold
elif RSI < 35: 
    oversold_score += 20  # deeply oversold
elif RSI < 45: 
    oversold_score += 10  # moderately oversold

# Bollinger Band (BB) position contribution
# BB_Position is 0 at lower band, 1 at upper band
if BB_Position < 0.15: 
    oversold_score += 25  # price touching lower band
elif BB_Position < 0.35: 
    oversold_score += 15  # price in lower half of band
elif BB_Position < 0.50: 
    oversold_score += 5   # below mid-band

# Volume anomaly contribution
volume_surge_ratio = current_volume / avg_volume_10d
if volume_surge_ratio > 1.5:
    oversold_score += 15  # huge volume spike (panic sell or big accumulation)
elif volume_surge_ratio > 1.2:
    oversold_score += 8   # moderate volume surge

# Cap the oversold_score at 100
oversold_score = min(100, oversold_score)
Interpretation: Multiple technical factors confirm oversold conditions:
RSI: Below 35 signals oversold; below 25 is extreme.
Bollinger Band Position: Price near the lower band (especially <0.15) indicates it‚Äôs at an extreme low relative to recent range.
Volume Surge: Unusually high volume during the drop can indicate panic selling (often preceding a bottom) or smart money accumulation.
These factors sum up to an oversold_score (higher = more oversold). A high oversold_score means the stock is technically primed for a rebound.
Step 3: Fundamental Health Check
Evaluate if the company‚Äôs fundamentals support a rebound. We compute a fundamental_confidence (0-100) score:
fundamental_confidence = 0

# Profitability and growth
if quarterly_earnings_growth_yoy > 15%: 
    fundamental_confidence += 25  # strong growth
elif quarterly_earnings_growth_yoy > 5%: 
    fundamental_confidence += 15  # moderate growth

if is_profitable: 
    fundamental_confidence += 10  # company is currently profitable

# Debt and balance sheet strength
if debt_to_equity < 0.5: 
    fundamental_confidence += 15  # low debt
elif debt_to_equity < 1.0: 
    fundamental_confidence += 8   # acceptable debt level

if net_worth_positive: 
    fundamental_confidence += 5   # not over-leveraged (shareholder equity > 0)

# Cash flow / shareholder rewards
if has_recent_dividend or share_buyback_announced: 
    fundamental_confidence += 10  # returning cash to shareholders signals strength

fundamental_confidence = min(100, fundamental_confidence)
Interpretation: We reward fundamentally solid stocks:
Growth: Rising earnings (YoY) suggest the price dip might be temporary.
Profitability: Profitable companies weather storms better than loss-making ones.
Debt Levels: Low debt gives the company flexibility; high debt is risky if a downturn continues.
Positive Net Worth: Ensures assets exceed liabilities (no insolvency risk).
Cash Returns: Dividends or buybacks imply management confidence in the company‚Äôs financial health.
A higher fundamental_confidence (closer to 100) means the stock has a strong fundamental backbone, making a rebound more likely and sustainable. If this score is very low or zero (e.g. negative growth, high debt), we likely avoid boosting even if technicals look good ‚Äì it could be a ‚Äúvalue trap.‚Äù
Step 4: Catalyst Check
Assess if there‚Äôs a bullish catalyst that could spark the rebound. We use an AI-generated news sentiment score (ai_score) and its certainty, plus check for any known upcoming events:
catalyst_strength = 0

# News sentiment score (already 0-100 scaled)
if ai_score >= 80: 
    catalyst_strength = 25   # extremely positive news flow
elif ai_score >= 70: 
    catalyst_strength = 18   # strong positive news
elif ai_score >= 60: 
    catalyst_strength = 12   # moderately positive
else: 
    catalyst_strength = 0    # no significant positive news (no catalyst)

# Confidence/Certainty adjustment
if catalyst_strength > 0:
    if news_certainty >= 0.8: 
        catalyst_strength += 10  # highly certain news impact
    elif news_certainty >= 0.6: 
        catalyst_strength += 5   # moderately certain
Additionally, if there‚Äôs a known upcoming catalyst (e.g. earnings release, product launch) and the stock meets the correction criteria, we could manually boost catalyst_strength (e.g. +5) to account for anticipated news. Interpretation: A positive news backdrop or catalyst greatly increases the odds of a rebound:
A high AI news sentiment score (e.g. ‚â• 70) means recent news coverage or analyst reports are bullish ‚Äì perhaps insider buying, new contracts, or industry accolades.
Certainty accounts for the confidence in that AI signal. High certainty with a positive score is a powerful combo.
Without any catalyst (score < 60), we usually skip the correction boost ‚Äì a stock might be oversold, but lacking a trigger, it could stay down for long.
Step 5: Calculate Correction Confidence
Combine the above factors into a unified correction_confidence (a multiplier between 0.0 and 1.0):
# Weighted combination of technical, fundamental, and catalyst signals
correction_confidence = (
    (0.3 * oversold_score) +         # 30% weight: technical oversold signals
    (0.3 * fundamental_confidence) + # 30% weight: fundamentals strength
    (0.4 * catalyst_strength)       # 40% weight: news/catalyst impact
) / 100.0  # normalize to 0-1 range

# Safety clamping
if correction_confidence > 1.0:
    correction_confidence = 1.0  # max confidence is 100%
We emphasize the catalyst slightly more (40% weight) since a positive catalyst can be the immediate trigger for reversal. Technical oversold conditions and fundamentals each contribute 30%. This weighting reflects that even a deeply oversold stock with great fundamentals may not rebound quickly without a catalyst, while a strong catalyst on an oversold stock with okay fundamentals can still spur a big bounce.
If correction_confidence is below 0.3: The signals are too weak or mixed ‚Üí no boost is applied (the stock doesn‚Äôt have enough going for it).
If ‚â• 0.3: We consider applying a boost proportional to this confidence.
Practical note: In a very bearish overall market, we may scale this confidence down a bit (see Market Context below). Conversely, in a strong bull market, even a 0.3 confidence might result in a rebound, but we stick to conservative thresholds to avoid false positives.
Step 6: Apply Score Boost
Finally, translate correction_confidence into extra points added to the stock‚Äôs score (the hybrid score that combines AI and technical scores):
# Calculate base hybrid score from AI and technical components (unchanged)
hybrid_score = (0.6 * ai_score) + (0.4 * technical_score)

# Determine boost factor based on confidence tiers
if correction_confidence >= 0.85:
    BOOST_FACTOR = 20  # very high confidence -> large boost
elif correction_confidence >= 0.70:
    BOOST_FACTOR = 15
elif correction_confidence >= 0.55:
    BOOST_FACTOR = 10
elif correction_confidence >= 0.40:
    BOOST_FACTOR = 5
else:
    BOOST_FACTOR = 0   # confidence too low -> no boost

# Apply a calibrated boost
confidence_boost = correction_confidence * BOOST_FACTOR

# Avoid overshooting the maximum score
final_score = min(100, hybrid_score + confidence_boost)
Key Points:
We use a tiered Boost Factor approach: higher confidence yields a bigger potential boost to the score (up to +20 points for the most compelling setups). For example, a correction_confidence of 0.60 (60%) gives a BOOST_FACTOR of 10, meaning up to +6 points (0.6 * 10) added.
Diminishing returns: To keep scores realistic, we ensure final_score ‚â§ 100. We can also optionally scale down the boost if the base hybrid_score is already very high. E.g. if a stock‚Äôs hybrid score is 85 on its own (already excellent), even a high confidence correction might warrant a slightly smaller incremental boost to avoid every top scorer hitting the cap. This prevents over-boosting stocks that are already highly rated by other means.
If confidence is marginal (e.g. 0.4), the boost is small (+2 points). If confidence is below 0.3, no boost is applied at all (the stock remains scored by the standard model).
üîç CONSOLIDATION & REVERSAL CONFIRMATION
One practical enhancement is verifying that the stock is no longer in a free-fall and is starting to stabilize or turn up. This prevents boosting a stock that‚Äôs oversold but still dropping (a ‚Äúfalling knife‚Äù). We incorporate a consolidation check and reversal signals:
# After a correction is detected:
consolidation_confirmed = False
reversal_signals = 0

# 1. Price consolidation check (e.g., over last 10 days)
trading_range = (max(close_prices[-10:]) - min(close_prices[-10:])) / current_price
if trading_range < 0.10:  # <10% range in last 2 weeks
    consolidation_confirmed = True  # price has flattened out

# 2. Simple trend reversal signal (e.g., moving average or price action)
if current_price > moving_avg_20: 
    reversal_signals += 1  # broke above short-term MA
if RSI > 50 and RSI_crossed_above_signal(): 
    reversal_signals += 1  # momentum turning up (RSI or MACD crossover)
if recent_candle_pattern == 'hammer' or recent_candle_pattern == 'morning_star':
    reversal_signals += 1  # bullish candlestick pattern detected

# Require at least two different reversal signals for high confidence
if consolidation_confirmed and reversal_signals >= 1:
    confirmed_reversal = True
elif reversal_signals >= 2:
    confirmed_reversal = True
else:
    confirmed_reversal = False
How we use this: If confirmed_reversal is False, we might delay or reduce the boost, even if other criteria look good. Essentially, the stock should exhibit some sign of bottoming:
Trading in a narrow range after a drop (consolidation) implies selling pressure is waning and buyers are stepping in at a support level.
A move above a short-term moving average (e.g. 20-day) or a bullish momentum indicator crossover suggests the downtrend is ending.
Recognizable bullish reversal candlestick patterns (like a hammer or a morning star on the chart) provide visual confirmation of a potential trend change.
Multiple signals give stronger confirmation. We don‚Äôt need all of them, but at least a couple increases reliability.
By requiring some confirmation, we make the boost more bulletproof ‚Äì focusing on stocks that not only should rebound, but are already starting to rebound as evidence by price action. This avoids boosting stocks that just keep falling despite being ‚Äúoversold.‚Äù
‚ö†Ô∏è RISK FILTERS & SAFETY CHECKS
Even if a stock scores well on correction and rebound signals, we enforce risk management filters to avoid problematic cases:
def passes_risk_filters(stock):
    # 1. Financial red flags
    if stock.debt_to_equity > 2.0 or stock.current_ratio < 0.8:
        return False  # too much debt or low liquidity - financially risky
    if stock.market_cap < 500: 
        return False  # very small company (‚ÇπCr) - avoid illiquid penny stocks
    
    # 2. Trading liquidity
    if stock.avg_daily_volume < 100000:
        return False  # not enough liquidity, skip boosting
    
    # 3. Volatility and stability
    if stock.beta > 1.5 and correction_confidence < 0.5:
        # Highly volatile stock and we have low confidence -> skip
        return False
    if stock.listed_months < 6:
        return False  # IPO or newly listed stock, not enough data for reliable patterns
    
    return True
Risk Criteria:
Excessive Debt / Weak Finances: If debt-to-equity is very high (>2) or current ratio (current assets/liabilities) is very low, the company might be in trouble regardless of technicals. We do not boost in such cases, since a rebound could be a bull trap.
Tiny Market Cap or Low Volume: Very small or illiquid stocks can swing wildly and are prone to manipulation. We focus on established stocks (e.g. market cap above ‚Çπ500 Cr and decent trading volume) where our signals are more reliable.
High Volatility: If a stock is extremely volatile (high beta) and our confidence is not very strong, we avoid boosting ‚Äì the price might be swinging on noise.
Insufficient History: If the stock has been trading for only a few months (e.g. recent IPO), there‚Äôs not enough historical data to trust our correction signals, so we skip it.
These filters ensure the strategy stays practical and safe, avoiding scenarios that don‚Äôt fit the typical correction-rebound pattern.
üåê MARKET CONTEXT ADJUSTMENT
A stock does not trade in isolation ‚Äì overall market and sector conditions matter. We adjust our strategy modestly based on the context:
Bullish Market: If the broader market trend is positive (e.g., index in uptrend, low volatility), more aggressive boosting is acceptable. In a bull market, oversold quality stocks tend to rebound faster. Implementation: We might lower the confidence threshold (e.g., use 0.25 instead of 0.30 as the cutoff) or slightly increase the BOOST_FACTOR for high confidence cases.
Bearish or Uncertain Market: If the market is in a downturn or highly volatile (e.g., VIX fear index is high, indices in correction), we tighten criteria. For example, require correction_confidence a bit higher (say 0.35) before any boost, and cap BOOST_FACTOR for all stocks (to maybe 10 points max). This accounts for the fact that in bear markets, even good stocks can continue falling or languish longer.
Sector Check: We also consider if the stock‚Äôs sector is particularly weak or strong. If the entire sector is under a negative spotlight (regulatory issues, cyclical downturn), we may temper the boost. Conversely, if the sector outlook is very strong, that adds conviction. Practically, this could mean adjusting correction_confidence up or down by ~10% based on sector performance indices or ETF trends.
This context-aware tweaking keeps the boost realistic. For instance, a 20% corrected stock in a booming tech sector (market friendly) is more likely to rebound than a 20% corrected stock in a sector facing headwinds ‚Äì our algorithm can reflect that by altering confidence slightly before applying the boost. The core formula remains the same, but these are multiplier adjustments on correction_confidence for an extra layer of prudence.
