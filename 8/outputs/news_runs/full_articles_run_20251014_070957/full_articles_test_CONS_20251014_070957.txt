Post earnings announcement, shares of the company rose 4% to the day's high of Rs 155.85 on the BSE.
Revenue from operations rose 26.2% YoY to Rs 2,057 crore, compared with Rs 1,629.55 crore in Q2 FY25, the company said in an exchange filing.
Asset quality improved on a sequential basis for the company. Gross Non-Performing Assets improved to 3.97% from 4.13% in the June quarter, while Net Non-Performing Assets improved to 1.97% from 2.06% in the previous quarter.
On a sequential basis, the net profit more than doubled, rising 122% from Rs 247 crore reported in the first quarter. Revenue over the same period also gained from Rs 1,960 crore, a 5% increase from the previous quarter.
In its business update that it released earlier this month, IREDA's loans sanctioned until September 30, 2025, increased by 86% from the same quarter last year to Rs 33,148 crore.
IREDA's disbursements increased by 54% on a year-on-year basis to Rs 15,043 crore, while its loan book outstanding at the end of the September quarter grew by 31% on a year-on-year basis to Rs 84,445 crore.
At about 11:45 am, shares of the company were trading at Rs 154.55 per share, higher by 3% from the last close on the NSE. The stock has fallen over 30% on a year-to-date basis. The stock touched an intra-day high of Rs 156, higher by 4% from the last close on the NSE.

Title   : India-Japan work pact to send 50,000 skilled professionals to Japan: Consul-General
Source  : www.thehindubusinessline.com
Published: 2025-10-13T15:55:39
Fetched : 2025-10-14T07:49:32.432233
URL     : https://www.thehindubusinessline.com/economy/india-japan-work-pact-to-send-50000-skilled-professionals-to-japan-consul-general/article70159523.ece
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The India-Japan Human Resource Exchange and Cooperation agreement signed in August of this year will be a great opportunity for Indian youth to develop advanced technical skills and work experience in Japan, according to Takahashi Muneo, Consul-General, The Consulate-General of Japan, Chennai.
Muneo was speaking at a prize distribution ceremony for the India Japan Intercollegiate Competitions 2025, organised by the Indo-Japan Chamber of Commerce and Industry here on Monday.
In his address, Muneo emphasised the need for collaboration between Indian and Japan not only in economy and the technological sector, but also in people-to-people exchange, education, and culture.
“The India-Japan Human Research Exchange and cooperation, outlined a roadmap for talent mobility and deeper people-to-people ties. Under the plan, we envision the exchange of more than 500 persons over the next five years, including 50,000 skilled professionals and potential talent from India to Japan,” he added.
Muneo added that initiatives such as the Japan Industrial Institute for Manufacturing and the Japan Industrial Course have helped create a strong talent pipeline of over 30,000 young professionals trained in Japan-style manufacturing and management
The event also saw the presence of N Ravi, Director, Kasturi and Sons, who acknowledged the deep history of partnership between the two countries and stressed on the need for further extending the collaboration in sectors such as defence, space research, clean energy technology, and cooperation.
