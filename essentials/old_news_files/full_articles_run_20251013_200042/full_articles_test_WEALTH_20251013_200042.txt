Anand Rathi Wealth reported a 31 per cent increase in consolidated profit for the quarter ended September 2025 at ₹99.89 crore as against ₹76.31 crore in the corresponding quarter last year.
The revenue from operations rose by 22.6 per cent to ₹297.36 crore (₹242.48 crore).
Consolidated AUM during the said period was at ₹91,568 crore as against ₹75,084 crore in Q2FY26.
During April-September 2025 (H1FY26), the holding company’s active client base grew by 16 per cent y-o-y to 12,781. Client attrition — measured by AUM lost — remained low at 0.18 per cent for H1 FY26, according to the company.
The company announced its first interim dividend of ₹6 per share for FY26. The record date is fixed as October 17, 2025, as per the stock exchange filing.
In addition, the board also approved incorporation of a subsidiary company in GIFT city, Gandhi Nagar, Gujarat.
The stock closed at ₹2932.80 against the previous close of ₹2955.65 on the BSE.
