BoM Q2 results live: bank will need about ₹2,500 crore extra provisioning to comply with ECL norms
Bank of Maharashtra (BoM) will require approximately ₹2,500 crore in additional provisioning to meet the expected credit loss (ECL) norms, stated MD & CEO Nidhu Saxena. The Reserve Bank of India plans to implement the ECL-based provisioning framework, replacing the current incurred-loss model, for banks starting 1st April 2027. Lenders will be provided a glide path until 31st March 2031 to ease any one-time impact of higher provisioning on their existing loan books.
