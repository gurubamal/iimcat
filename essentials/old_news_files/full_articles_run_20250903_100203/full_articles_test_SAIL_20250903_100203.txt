Bengaluru
Equity benchmarks staged volatility as investors kept a close watch on the outcome of the GST Council meeting. Both the Sensex and Nifty fluctuated between gains and losses amid the cautious sentiment.
Sensex was up 36.05 pts or 0.04 per cent at 80,193.93 as at 1.13 pm (off the day’s high of 80,344.98). The index touched an intraday low of 80,004.60. Nifty 50 inched up 24.55 pts or 0.1 per cent to 24,604.15.
Smallcaps outshone midcaps in today’s trade. On the sectoral front, metal stocks soared nearly 3 per cent. All other indices except IT and telecom traded in the positive territory.
Among the Sensex firms, Tata Steel zoomed 5 per cent, Coal India, JSW Steel, Hindalco and Titan followed with 1-3 per cent gain.
On the flip side, Infosys, Axis Bank, Tech Mahindra, Wipro and Trent declined the most.
The equity market remained buoyant at 12.55 pm on the National Stock Exchange, with broad-based participation as 2,997 stocks were traded. Market breadth was firm with 2,025 advances outpacing 885 declines, while 87 stocks remained unchanged.
Manappuram, Bosch and Apollo Hositals featured among 75 stocks scaling fresh 52-week highs, and 31 stocks, including Indus Towers, hit a 52-week low.
In addition, 94 counters, such as E2E Networks and Hind Rectifiers, hit their upper circuit limits, against 40 in the lower circuit, including Uravi Defence.
All metal stocks except JSL traded in green at the time of writing. MIDHANI, Cyient DLM, Solar Industries and Unimech led the gainers of the defence index.
Under the midcap segment, SAIL, Yes Bank, Glenmark Pharma, National Aluminium and Muthoot Finance gained 2-4 per cent, while Phoenix Mills, Aditya Birla Fashion and Retail, MRF, Indus Towers, Coforge and Mphasis declined 1-3 per cent.
Shares of PPL Pharma zomed 7 per cent under the smallcap segment, Tejas Networks, ITI, Hindustan Copper, Welspun Corp and Shyam Metalics increased 3-5 per cent. Zensar Tech, Aster DM, HBL Engineering, GRSE and Ircon International declined by 2 per cent.
On the BSE, TBO TEK, Netweb, Delta Corp and TD Power Systems rallied 8-14 per cent, while Globus Spirits, AVL, Sharda Corp, Intellect Design and Phoenix Mills dragged 3-8 per cent.
